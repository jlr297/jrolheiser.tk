Welcome to my homepage! Hopefully if you are visiting this you are wanting to find out a little bit about me. I am Jesse Rolheiser, originally from small town Allan Saskatchewan (if you know anyone from there, yes I know them). I moved to Saskatoon in the Fall of 2011 to start my Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science.

My interest in programming started early when I began playing around with RPG maker 2000. Inspired from watching my older brother play the Final Fantasy games on the SNES, I wanted to create a game for him to play. I had no idea the scope of what I was wanting to accomplish, but the experience gave me my first taste into computer science.

Once I had taken CMPT 111 at the University of Saskatchewan, I was hooked and there was no turning back. I have studied a wide variety of topics in computer science over the past 4 years ranging from Web/Mobile programming and Information Security to Artificial Intelligence and Software Engineering. However, my primary focus and passion has been the study of Algorithms and Complexity theory, and I have taken a moderate amount of Math courses to compliment this passion.

Currently, I am the President of the Computer Science Students' Society. As well, I work part time in the HCI lab doing research in multi-surface applications for Dr. Carl Gutwin. My other interests that do not involve a monitor include Volleyball which I have been playing for 9 years. I have been curling for 10 years and currently curl in the Sutherland men's league. I have a strong passion for medieval fantasy novels, and a collection of a dozen Rubik's cubes.

I hope this gives you a quick idea of who I am and if you want to know more, stop and chat with me in Spinks or take a look at my projects and resume. I am always available via e-mail. Cheers!
